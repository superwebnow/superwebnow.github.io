Staal continued to develop into a top player in the NHL, and was named an alternate captain of the Penguins in 2008. He helped the Penguins win the Stanley Cup in 2009, scoring the winning goal in Game 2 of the Final. He scored a career-high 45 goals in the 2010–11 season, and was nominated for the Selke Trophy as the NHL’s best defensive forward.
After the NHL lockout, Staal scored 15 goals in the shortened 2012–13 season. He missed the majority of the 2013–14 season after suffering a knee injury. He returned to the lineup late in the season, and helped the Penguins reach the Stanley Cup Playoffs.
