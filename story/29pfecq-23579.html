Top Facts about Bethann Hardison

1. Bethann Hardison was born in Brooklyn, New York.
2. She was discovered by African American designer Willi Smith and began working for Smith as a fitting model.
3. Hardison was among the first high profile black models after appearing at the 1973 Battle of Versailles fashion show.
4. She co-founded the Black Girls Coalition in1988 to provide advocacy and support to African American models.
5. In 1999, she was honored with the First Annual Vibe Style Lifetime Achievement Award.
6. The Black Alumni of the Pratt Institute honored Hardison with a 2003 Lifetime Achievement award.
7. She was presented with a Distinguished Service Award from the Magic Johnson Foundation in 1999.
8. In 2012, she received a Woman of Power Legacy Award from Black Enterprise.
9. In 2013, Hardison became a Frederick Douglass award recipient for her work in promoting diversity in fashion.
10. In 2014, Hardison received the CFDA Founders Award.
11. In 2023, Hardison released Invisible Beauty, a documentary about her career and activism.
12. She works as a consultant for Gucci and the CFDA (as of April 2019).
13. She attended the New York University Art School and the Fashion Institute of Technology but did not graduate from either.
14. For a brief period Hardison worked as a corrections officer at Bedford Hills Correctional Facility.
15. During the 1960s, Hardison worked in the garment district of New York City as saleswoman.
