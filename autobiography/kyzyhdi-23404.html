Carter was a candidate in Carter for the Virginia House of Delegates for the 50th District. He was supported by the Democratic Socialists of America (DSA) of which he is an active member since April 2017. The campaign he ran on was focused mostly on issues like single-payer health care and financial donations to political figures. Jackson Miller, the incumbent Republican ran a mailer campaign which made comparisons of Carter to Communist leaders Vladimir Lenin, Joseph Stalin and Mao Zedong, an act that the Democratic Party of Virginia condemned as a smear campaign. Miller said Carter an “anti-jobs candidate” and said his “ideas are so out of the mainstream, and so incredibly expensive”. On the 7th of November in 2017, Carter won the race by nine percent. Carter was among 15 DSA members elected in 2017.

Carter ran for reelection in the 2019 election, having defeated his primary opponent, Manassas city councilman Mark Wolfe, by 57.7% to 42.3% of the vote.
